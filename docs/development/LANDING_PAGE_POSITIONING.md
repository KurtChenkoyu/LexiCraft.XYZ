## Executive Summary

This document captures critical positioning and messaging clarifications that fundamentally change how the landing page should be presented. The platform is **NOT a teaching app** - it is an **assessment/verification platform** that helps parents verify if their education investments (school, cram school) actually resulted in their children learning foundational language elements.

## Key Positioning Shifts

**Previous Positioning (INCORRECT)**:
- "Kids learn vocabulary and earn money"
- Teaching-focused messaging
- Implies we teach English

**Correct Positioning**:
- **We are an assessment/verification platform**
- We verify if children have encountered and can recognize foundational language elements
- We do NOT teach - children learn elsewhere (school, cram school)

**Key Message**:
> "We don't teach English, we verify if your child recognizes foundational language elements they were supposed to learn at school/cram school."

### 2. Target Audience Pain Point

**The Real Problem**:
- Parents invest heavily in education (school, cram school)
- Question: "Did my child actually learn anything?"
- Traditional tests don't tell parents if children truly understand or just memorized
- Uncertainty about ROI on education investments

**Our Solution**:
- Better assessment that verifies foundational language learning
- Clear visibility into what children actually recognize vs. what they haven't encountered
- Honest about what we can verify (recognition/exposure) vs. what we can't (deep understanding)

### 3. Hidden Vocabulary Gap: Critical Parent Education ‚ö†Ô∏è

**The Hidden Problem Many Parents Don't Know**:

- **Official Numbers**: Senior high curriculum says 4,000-5,000 words

- **Impact**: Students who only meet the "official" 4,000-5,000 words are actually behind

- Parents think their child is on track if they know 4,000-5,000 words
- But the system actually expects 8,000+
- This creates a hidden disadvantage
- **It's critical to stay way ahead of the suggested numbers**

**How We Address This**:

**Key Insight**:

### 6. Words vs. Learning Points (Core Differentiator)

**Critical Distinction to Explain**:

**Traditional "Words" Approach**:
- One word = one item
- Alphabetical order: apple, bank, cat...
- Only tests most common meaning
- Isolated testing, no connections
- Doesn't show how words form phrases

**Our "Learning Points" Approach**:
- One learning point = one verifiable language element
- Includes: words (in different contexts), phrases/collocations, idioms, morphological components, relationships
- Organized by language relationships, not alphabetical
- Connection testing - see how words connect
- Tests different context meanings
- Shows how words form phrases

**Example: "bank"**
- Traditional: 1 word = "bank" (financial institution)
- Learning Points: 
  - "bank" (financial) - Learning Point #1
  - "bank" (geographic/river) - Learning Point #2
  - "bank account" (phrase) - Learning Point #3
  - "river bank" (phrase) - Learning Point #4
  - bank ‚Üî account (relationship) - Learning Point #5
  - bank ‚Üî river (relationship) - Learning Point #6

**Why This Matters**:
- More complete picture of what children recognize
- More sensible structure (language relationships vs. alphabetical)
- More practical (know how words are actually used)
- More accurate (test different contexts, not just one)
