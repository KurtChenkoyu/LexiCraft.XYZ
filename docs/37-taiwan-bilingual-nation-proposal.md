## What "Verified Mastery" Means

### LexiCraft Verification System

LexiCraft's verification goes beyond simple testing:

1. **MCQ Verification**
   - 6-option multiple choice questions
   - 99.54% statistical confidence
   - Adaptive difficulty matching
   - 20-30 distractor pool per sense

2. **Spaced Repetition (FSRS/SM-2+)**
   - Initial verification: Day 1
   - First review: Day 3
   - Second review: Day 7
   - Subsequent reviews: Adaptive (14, 30, 60+ days)
   - Ensures long-term retention, not just short-term memorization

3. **Mastery Progression**
   - Learning ‚Üí Familiar ‚Üí Known ‚Üí Mastered
   - Multiple verification cycles required
   - Anti-gaming measures (speed traps, daily limits)

4. **Deep Understanding Requirements**
   - Context-aware questions
   - Multiple meanings tested (polysemy)
   - Confusion pairs addressed (e.g., affect/effect)
   - Real-world usage examples

### Why This Matters

Unlike traditional vocabulary tests that measure recognition, LexiCraft verifies:
- **Recognition** (can identify the word)
- **Comprehension** (understands meaning in context)
- **Retention** (remembers after days/weeks)
- **Application** (can distinguish from similar words)

## Phased Rollout Strategies

### Strategy A: Geographic Phased Rollout (3 Years)

**Year 1: Pilot Phase (10% of population)**
- Target: 2.35 million people
- Cost: 4.7 billion TWD
- Focus: Major cities (Taipei, Taichung, Kaohsiung)
- Goals:
  - Validate infrastructure at scale
  - Refine verification processes
  - Gather user feedback
  - Establish success metrics

**Year 2: Expansion Phase (Additional 10%)**
- Target: Additional 2.35 million people
- Cost: 4.7 billion TWD
- Focus: Secondary cities + Year 1 participants continue
- Goals:
  - Scale proven model
  - Expand to suburban areas
  - Continue Year 1 participants' progress

**Year 3: Completion Phase (Final 10%)**
- Target: Final 2.35 million people
- Cost: 4.7 billion TWD
- Focus: Rural areas + all previous participants
- Goals:
  - Complete geographic coverage
  - Support all participants to completion
  - Establish maintenance model

**Total Timeline:** 3 years  
**Total Cost:** 14.1 billion TWD  
**Ongoing Costs:** Maintenance and spaced repetition reviews

### Strategy B: Age-Group Phased Rollout (5 Years)

**Year 1: Elementary School (Ages 9-12)**
- Target: ~1.4 million students
- Cost: 2.8 billion TWD
- Focus: Foundation vocabulary (CEFR A1-A2)

**Year 2: Middle School (Ages 13-15)**
- Target: ~1.0 million students
- Cost: 2.0 billion TWD
- Focus: Intermediate vocabulary (CEFR B1)

**Year 3: High School (Ages 16-18)**
- Target: ~0.9 million students
- Cost: 1.8 billion TWD
- Focus: Advanced vocabulary (CEFR B2)

**Year 4: University/Adult (Ages 19-25)**
- Target: ~1.5 million people
- Cost: 3.0 billion TWD
- Focus: Academic/professional vocabulary

**Year 5: General Population (Ages 26+)**
- Target: ~2.25 million people
- Cost: 4.5 billion TWD
- Focus: Complete remaining population

**Total Timeline:** 5 years  
**Total Cost:** 14.1 billion TWD  
**Advantage:** Age-appropriate vocabulary targeting

### Strategy C: Hybrid Approach (Recommended)

**Phase 1: Pilot (Year 1)**
- 5% of population (1.175M) across all age groups
- Cost: 2.35 billion TWD
- Validate model, gather data

**Phase 2: School Integration (Years 2-3)**
- All K-12 students (~3.3M)
- Cost: 6.6 billion TWD
- Integrate into school curriculum

**Phase 3: Adult Population (Years 4-5)**
- Remaining 2.575M adults
- Cost: 5.15 billion TWD
- Voluntary participation, workplace programs

**Total Timeline:** 5 years

### Current LexiCraft Model vs. Government Model

```

  ‚Üì

  ‚Üì
Child earns points (90% of deposit)
  ‚Üì

```

**Revenue per User:** ~100-200 TWD  
**Platform Margin:** 8-10%

#### Government Model (B2G)
```
Government pays 1 TWD per sense verified
  ‚Üì
Student completes verification
  ‚Üì
Platform provides verification service
  ‚Üì
Platform margin: Minimal (public service)
```

**Revenue per User:** 2,000 TWD (2,000 senses √ó 1 TWD)  
**Platform Margin:** Must cover costs (potentially break-even or small margin)

### Break-Even Analysis

**Assumptions:**
- 2,000 TWD per person (2,000 senses √ó 1 TWD)
- Infrastructure: 1.15B TWD/year
- Personnel: 0.47-0.82B TWD/year
- Other: 0.3-0.4B TWD/year
- **Total Annual Operating:** 1.92-2.37B TWD/year

**Break-Even Calculation:**
- If 1 TWD/sense covers only verification costs: Need additional funding
- If 1 TWD/sense includes platform overhead: May be viable
- If government provides infrastructure support: More viable

**Recommendation:** Government should consider:
1. **Infrastructure subsidy** (separate from per-sense cost)
2. **Phased payment** (pay as verification completes, not upfront)
3. **Performance-based pricing** (bonus for high completion rates)
