## 1. Company Types in Taiwan

### 1.1 Limited Company (有限公司) - Recommended for MVP

**Best for:** Small businesses, startups, MVP stage

**Characteristics:**
- Minimum capital: NT$250,000 (~$8,000 USD)
- 1-50 shareholders
- Limited liability
- Simpler registration process
- Lower compliance requirements

**Requirements:**
- At least 1 shareholder (can be foreign)
- At least 1 director (can be foreign)
- Registered address in Taiwan
- Company name approval
- Articles of incorporation

**Pros:**
- ✅ Lower minimum capital
- ✅ Faster registration (2-4 weeks)
- ✅ Lower ongoing costs
- ✅ Suitable for beta/MVP stage

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Cannot issue shares publicly
- ⚠️ Limited to 50 shareholders

### 1.2 Company Limited by Shares (股份有限公司)

**Best for:** Larger businesses, future scaling

**Characteristics:**
- Minimum capital: NT$1,000,000 (~$32,000 USD)
- 2+ shareholders (can be 1 if publicly listed)
- Can issue shares
- More complex compliance

**Requirements:**
- At least 2 shareholders (or 1 if publicly listed)
- Board of directors (at least 3 directors)
- Registered address in Taiwan
- More detailed reporting requirements

**Pros:**
- ✅ Can issue shares
- ✅ Better for raising capital
- ✅ More credibility

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Higher minimum capital
- ⚠️ More complex registration (4-6 weeks)
- ⚠️ Higher ongoing compliance costs

### 1.3 Branch Office (分公司)

**Best for:** Foreign companies expanding to Taiwan

**Characteristics:**
- Extension of foreign parent company
- No separate legal entity
- Parent company liable for debts
- Requires parent company registration documents

**Requirements:**
- Foreign parent company must exist
- Parent company registration documents (translated)
- Registered address in Taiwan
- Representative in Taiwan

**Pros:**
- ✅ No separate capital requirement
- ✅ Can use parent company name

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Parent company fully liable
- ⚠️ Requires foreign company setup first
- ⚠️ More complex tax structure

### 1.4 Representative Office (代表處)

**Best for:** Market research, no business activities

**Characteristics:**
- Cannot conduct business activities
- Cannot sign contracts
- Cannot receive payments
- Limited to market research and promotion

**Requirements:**
- Foreign parent company
- Cannot generate revenue
- Limited activities

**Not suitable for LexiCraft** - Cannot accept payments

## 2. Registration Process

### 2.1 Pre-Registration Steps

#### Step 1: Company Name Reservation

**Process:**
1. Submit company name application to MOEA (Ministry of Economic Affairs)
2. Check name availability (cannot duplicate existing names)
3. Name approval (usually 1-2 business days)
4. Name reservation valid for 6 months

**Cost:** NT$300 (~$10 USD)

**Documents Required:**
- Company name application form
- Name options (3-5 names, ranked by preference)

#### Step 2: Prepare Registration Documents

**Required Documents:**

1. **Articles of Incorporation (公司章程)**
   - Company name
   - Business scope
   - Capital amount
   - Shareholder information
   - Director information
   - Registered address

2. **Shareholder List (股東名冊)**
   - Name, ID number, address
   - Shareholding percentage
   - Capital contribution

3. **Director List (董事名冊)**
   - Name, ID number, address
   - Director appointment

4. **Registered Address Proof (公司地址證明)**
   - Lease agreement or property ownership
   - Address must be in Taiwan

5. **Capital Verification (資本額查核)**
   - Bank statement showing capital deposit
   - CPA verification report (if capital > NT$5M)

6. **Identity Documents**
   - For Taiwanese: National ID
   - For foreigners: Passport + ARC (Alien Resident Certificate) or visa

#### Step 3: Capital Deposit

**Process:**
1. Open temporary bank account in company name (after name approval)
2. Deposit minimum capital (NT$250,000 for Limited Company)
3. Obtain bank statement
4. If capital > NT$5M, hire CPA for verification

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

**Cost:**
- Bank account opening: NT$1,000-3,000
- CPA verification (if needed): NT$10,000-30,000

### 2.2 Registration Application

#### Step 4: Submit to MOEA

**Process:**
1. Submit all documents to MOEA
2. Pay registration fee
3. Wait for approval (usually 5-10 business days)
4. Receive company registration certificate

**Documents to Submit:**
- Articles of incorporation
- Shareholder list
- Director list
- Registered address proof
- Capital verification documents
- Application form
- Registration fee payment receipt

**Cost:** NT$1,000 (~$32 USD)

**Timeline:** 5-10 business days

#### Step 5: Post-Registration Steps

**After receiving registration certificate:**

1. **Tax Registration (稅籍登記)**
   - Register with National Taxation Bureau
   - Obtain tax ID number
   - Timeline: 1-2 weeks
   - Cost: Free

2. **Business License (if required)**
   - Some industries require additional licenses
   - For EdTech/online education: May not require special license
   - Check with MOEA for specific requirements

3. **Open Business Bank Account**
   - Use company registration certificate
   - Required for Stripe/payment processing
   - Timeline: 1-2 weeks
   - Cost: NT$1,000-3,000

4. **Employee Insurance (if hiring)**
   - Register with Labor Insurance Bureau
   - Register with National Health Insurance
   - Required if hiring employees

## 3. Foreign Company Registration

### 3.1 Requirements for Foreign Shareholders

**If you're a foreign individual:**
- Valid passport
- ARC (Alien Resident Certificate) or valid visa
- Proof of address in Taiwan or home country
- Can be shareholder and director

**If you're a foreign company:**
- Foreign company registration documents
- Translated and notarized
- Company seal/certificate
- Representative appointment

### 3.2 Common Structures

**Option 1: Foreign Individual as Shareholder**
- Foreigner can be 100% shareholder
- Foreigner can be director
- Requires ARC or valid visa
- Simpler structure

**Option 2: Foreign Company as Shareholder**
- Foreign company owns Taiwan company
- More complex documentation
- Requires foreign company setup first
- Better for international operations

**Option 3: Mixed (Foreign + Local)**
- Foreign and local shareholders
- Can leverage local partner's knowledge
- More complex but may have advantages

## 5. Timeline

### 5.1 Registration Timeline

| Step | Duration | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Company name reservation | 1-2 days | Fast if name available |
| Prepare documents | 1-2 weeks | Depends on complexity |
| Capital deposit | 1 week | Bank account opening |
| CPA verification (if needed) | 1 week | Only if capital >NT$5M |
| MOEA registration | 5-10 days | Government processing |
| Tax registration | 1-2 weeks | After MOEA approval |
| Business bank account | 1-2 weeks | After registration |
| **Total (DIY)** | **4-6 weeks** | Minimum |
| **Total (with service)** | **3-4 weeks** | With professional help |

### 5.2 Factors Affecting Timeline

- **Document preparation:** Faster if you have all documents ready
- **Name availability:** Slower if preferred names are taken
- **Foreign documents:** Slower if translation/notarization needed
- **Government processing:** Can vary by season/workload
- **Professional service:** Faster with experienced service provider

## 6. Business Scope for LexiCraft

### 6.1 Recommended Business Scope

**For Articles of Incorporation, include:**

```
1. Online education platform development and operation
2. Educational software development
3. Online learning services
4. Digital content creation and distribution
5. Payment processing services (as agent)
6. Data processing and analysis services
7. Internet information services
8. Software development and sales
```

**Why broad scope:**
- Allows flexibility for future features
- Covers payment processing activities
- Includes educational technology services
- Avoids need for scope amendments later

### 6.2 Special Licenses Required?

**For LexiCraft operations:**
- **Online education:** Generally no special license required
- **Payment processing:** Using Stripe (licensed processor) - no additional license needed if under NT$2B threshold
- **Data processing:** No special license if compliant with PDPA
- **Check with MOEA:** Confirm specific requirements for your business model

## 7. Registered Address Options

### 7.1 Options

**Option 1: Own Property**
- Use your own property address
- No ongoing cost
- Must be in Taiwan

**Option 2: Rent Office**
- Rent commercial office space
- Cost: NT$12,000-60,000/month
- More professional appearance
- Can use for business operations

**Option 3: Virtual Office/Registered Address Service**
- Use service provider's address
- Cost: NT$1,000-3,000/month
- Cannot use for business operations
- Suitable for online-only businesses

**Option 4: Co-working Space**
- Rent desk/office in co-working space
- Cost: NT$5,000-20,000/month
- Can use address for registration
- Can use space for operations

### 7.2 Recommendation for LexiCraft

**For Beta/MVP:**
- Use virtual office or registered address service
- Cost-effective (NT$1,000-3,000/month)
- Sufficient for online-only business
- Can upgrade to physical office later

## 8. Tax Considerations

### 8.1 Corporate Income Tax

**Rate:** 20% on net taxable income

**Filing:**
- Annual tax return (May 1-31)
- Quarterly provisional tax payments (if applicable)
- Monthly VAT returns (if registered for VAT)

### 8.2 VAT (Value Added Tax)

**Rate:** 5%

**Registration threshold:** NT$480,000 annual revenue

**For LexiCraft:**
- May need to register if revenue exceeds threshold
- Online services may be subject to VAT
- Consult tax advisor for specific requirements

### 8.3 Withholding Tax

**For foreign shareholders:**
- Dividend payments: 21% withholding tax (or lower if tax treaty)
- Consult tax advisor for treaty benefits

## 9. Ongoing Compliance Requirements

### 9.1 Annual Requirements

1. **Annual Report Filing**
   - File annual report with MOEA
   - Update shareholder/director information if changed
   - Deadline: Within 1 month of fiscal year end
   - Cost: Free

2. **Tax Filing**
   - Corporate income tax return
   - VAT returns (if registered)
   - Deadline: May 1-31 (for previous year)
   - Cost: Free if DIY, NT$30,000-100,000 if outsourced

3. **Accounting Records**
   - Maintain proper accounting records
   - Keep invoices and receipts
   - Required for tax filing

4. **Registered Address**
   - Must maintain valid registered address
   - Update MOEA if address changes

### 9.2 Change Reporting

**Must report to MOEA within 30 days if:**
- Change of registered address
- Change of directors
- Change of shareholders (if significant)
- Change of capital (increase/decrease)
- Change of business scope

### 10.1 Recommended Structure

**For Beta/MVP Stage:**

1. **Company Type:** Limited Company (有限公司)
   - Lower capital requirement (NT$250,000)

## 11. Alternative: Using Existing Entity

### 11.1 Using Existing Cram School Entity

**If you already have a Taiwan company:**

**Pros:**
- ✅ No registration needed
- ✅ Already has tax ID
- ✅ Already has bank account
- ✅ Can start accepting payments immediately

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Business scope may need updating
- ⚠️ May need to add business activities
- ⚠️ Liability tied to existing entity

**Process:**
1. Check if business scope covers online education
2. If not, file scope amendment with MOEA
3. Use existing bank account for Stripe
4. Start accepting payments

**Cost:** NT$1,000 (scope amendment fee) + legal review

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

### 11.2 Recommendation

**If you have existing entity:**
- Use it for beta testing
- Faster to market (1-2 weeks vs 6-7 weeks)
- Lower cost
- Can register new entity later if needed

**If you don't have existing entity:**
- Register new Limited Company
- Follow timeline above
- Use professional service for first registration
